The Challenge

Why Fire System Failures Are So Preventable — Yet So Common

Most fire system failures are not equipment failures — they are design, installation and maintenance failures. Specifying the wrong detector type, inadequate coverage, poor cable management or skipped servicing all create life-safety risk that regulators and insurers will not tolerate.

Wrong Detector for the Environment

Ionisation detectors in kitchens. Optical detectors in dusty warehouses. Heat detectors in areas where smoke detectors are essential. Misspecification causes both missed detections and chronic false alarms — neither is acceptable in a life-safety system.

Inadequate Coverage Planning

A compliant number of detectors on paper does not guarantee compliant coverage in practice. Ceiling height, roof pitch, air movement and obstruction all affect detector placement — and only a proper design survey identifies every gap.

False Alarm Fatigue

Systems that generate frequent false alarms quickly lose the confidence of staff and management. When the alarm sounds, people hesitate. That hesitation is the difference between a controlled evacuation and a tragedy.

Maintenance Neglect

Fire alarm systems require regular testing and servicing to remain BS 5839 compliant and insurable. A system that has not been serviced is a system whose performance in a real fire is unknown — and unknowable.
