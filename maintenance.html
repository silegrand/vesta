The Challenge

Why Security Systems Fail — and Why Maintenance Prevents It

Security infrastructure that is installed and forgotten is security infrastructure that will eventually fail. Cameras drift out of focus. Hard drives fill up silently. Access card readers fail intermittently. Wireless access points drop into legacy modes. None of these failures announce themselves — they are discovered only when the system is needed most.

CCTV Systems Degrade Silently

Lens contamination, camera misalignment, NVR storage failures and firmware vulnerabilities all develop gradually. Without regular inspection and testing, a CCTV system that appears to be working may be recording nothing useful — and you will not know until an incident investigation reveals the gap.

Access Control Failures Create Security Vulnerabilities

A door controller that fails open is a security breach. A reader that intermittently fails triggers repeated calls to helpdesks and workarounds that undermine the entire access policy. Regular testing and firmware management prevent both.

Wireless Networks Drift Over Time

RF environments change as buildings and equipment change. Access points that provided excellent coverage at installation can develop coverage gaps as interference sources are introduced. Without proactive monitoring and periodic surveys, wireless degradation goes unnoticed until productivity suffers.

Reactive Support Is Always More Expensive

Waiting for systems to fail before calling an engineer means longer downtime, higher call-out costs and the possibility of a security gap during the repair window. A PPM contract with priority response turns reactive emergencies into managed, predictable costs.
